## Article

1. **Role Description** – A focal for all the procurement of a thematic custom random map pool intended for competitive Age of Empires II gameplay. The {Map Organizer} balances novelty and thematic immersion with classical competitive Age of Empires II using established Alchemy AOE Community procedures and design standards.

2. **Responsibilities** – {Map Organizer}s facilitate the creation and execution of thematic custom map pools for Alchemy Alliance competitions within Age of Empires II.

   <ol type="A">
     <li><strong>Before Contract Award</strong> – Prior to consideration of a thematic map pool featured in an Alchemy Alliance competition, the following activities are typically performed by {Map Organizer}s:</li>
       <ol type="1">
         <li>Drafts the request for proposals from the random map scripting community (or recruits scripters directly), describing the theme of the custom map pool, and then reviews responses.</li>
         <li>Fits map concepts of different types/styles to form a strategically diverse yet thematically appropriate collection.</li>
         <li>Prepares specifications for custom random maps fully defining the needs of the competition (such as thematic appropriateness, readability, strategic variety, etc.) while at the same time leaving freedom for random map creators to execute their visions.</li>
       </ol>
       There is no obligation to perform any of the above tasks within a specific timeframe (or at all), since no Alchemy Alliance resources are committed to executing support work.

     <li><strong>To Be Awarded Contract</strong> – {Map Organizer}s engage in the following to assure that their custom random map collections are featured in an Alchemy Alliance competition:</li>
       <ol type="1">
         <li>Present map specifications and creation team to {[Tournament Host](https://github.com/Alchemy-AOE-Community/CHEM-Charter/blob/main/CH-15-Tournament-Host.md)}s.</li>
         <li>Persuade {[Tournament Host](https://github.com/Alchemy-AOE-Community/CHEM-Charter/blob/main/CH-15-Tournament-Host.md)}s through “water‑tight” definitions, reasonable timetables, reliable creators/evaluators, and creative vision.</li>
       </ol>

     <li><strong>After Contract Award</strong> – If a contract is awarded within the proposed timeline, then the {Map Organizer} takes on the following additional responsibilities:</li>
       <ol type="1">
         <li>Track milestones of map project completion, providing support and solutions for any that may be falling behind schedule.</li>
         <li>Appoint a team and/or personally review random custom maps against their applicable specifications, identifying and helping to resolve any non‑conformities.</li>
         <li>Independently engage in correspondence with Alchemy AOE Community resources to achieve any of the above points.</li>
       </ol>
       Depending on level of experience, the above tasks may receive varying levels of support/guidance from the {[Tournament Host](https://github.com/Alchemy-AOE-Community/CHEM-Charter/blob/main/CH-15-Tournament-Host.md)}s. If a contract is awarded but the {Map Organizer} fails to deliver, then they may be removed.
   </ol>

3. **Time Commitment** – Estimated time required in the following relevant activities:

   <ol type="A">
     <li><strong>Specification Package</strong> – On average, each specification requires approximately 2 hours to complete for an experienced {Map Organizer}. At least three specifications are required (typically 2 plus the number of maps in the collection).</li>
       <ol type="1">
         <li>A single procurement specification defining map list and creators.</li>
         <li>At least one component specification defining the characteristics of a map within the list.</li>
         <li>At least one general specification defining common characteristics among all included maps.</li>
       </ol>
       The typical map collection may range from 8–12 thematic pieces, for a total of 20–28 hours invested in drafting specifications. While this may seem large, experience in map procurement has demonstrated that for every hour the Map Organizer spends defining requirements, the community (combined efforts of creators, playtesters, inspectors, etc.) will spend no fewer than ten hours assuring that the map meets its thematic vision and Alchemy Alliance quality standards.

     <li><strong>Map Pack Coordination</strong> – Depending on project complexity and guidance, the {Map Organizer} can expect to invest between 1–3 hours per week, for 6–12 weeks, coordinating Alchemy Alliance resources to create, playtest, inspect, etc. custom random maps.</li>
   </ol>

4. **Benefits** – The Alchemy Alliance is led by servant leaders and volunteers. There is no monetary compensation available to the {Map Organizer}, but some reasons to help are listed below:

   <ol type="A">
     <li><strong>Experience</strong> – Leadership experience with opportunity to learn about RMS engineering, configuration management, and procurement processes.</li>
     <li><strong>Notoriety</strong> – Recognition from organizing custom maps for an Alchemy Alliance tournament.</li>
     <li><strong>Preview Access</strong> – Access to the Alchemy Alliance native specifications repository entails preview of other projects, with occasional influence over projects/procedures unrelated to any map collection.</li>
     <li><strong>Manifestation</strong> – Ability to make an AoE2 dream a reality. The privilege of deciding where the Alchemy League goes and what maps are played on. “If you’ve always wanted to see it happen in the AoE2 universe, this is your chance to make it a reality.”</li>
   </ol>

5. **Qualifications** – There is no limit to the number of persons within the Alchemy Alliance forum that may hold the {Map Organizer} role:

   <ol type="A">
     <li>Be an Alchemy Affiliate per [CH-06](https://github.com/Alchemy-AOE-Community/CHEM-Charter/blob/main/CH-06-Affiliate.md).</li>
     <li>Have a free [GitHub Account](https://github.com).</li>
     <li>Champion the belief that proper planning and definition of requirements/expectations among community leaders can mitigate or entirely prevent the kinds of problems experienced by large, diverse, and complex online communities.</li>
     <li>Are a self‑starter with initiative and the proactivity to anticipate technical challenges; eager to learn about custom random map creation and tournament organization; unafraid to reach out to other Alchemy AOE volunteers.</li>
     <li>Speak English and are responsive to correspondence.</li>
     <li>Have a compelling vision for where the Alchemy AOE Community will go for a competition.</li>
   </ol>

6. **Desired Qualities** – It would be an advantage to any applicant of this role if they ALSO had:

   <ol type="A">
     <li>Experience organizing or administering AOE2 tournaments.</li>
     <li>Experience developing random custom maps, particularly those intended for use in a competitive AOE2 setting.</li>
     <li>Technical writing experience.</li>
     <li>Any kind of engineering experience.</li>
     <li>Knowledge of skilled AOE2 gameplay and an ability to predict how a map would “play out” in a competitive setting.</li>
     <li>A unique idea for a theme in an Alchemy Alliance competition, distinct from previous settings.</li>
     <li>Time zone close to East US.</li>
   </ol>

7. **On/Off Boarding** – per [CH-06](https://github.com/Alchemy-AOE-Community/CHEM-Charter/blob/main/CH-06-Affiliate.md).
